Who We Are

Sarawak Forestry Corporation (SFC) is a government agency dedicated to managing Totally Protected Areas (TPAs) and conserving biodiversity in Sarawak.

Our Mission

  • Protect and manage national parks, wildlife sanctuaries, and nature reserves.
  • Safeguard Sarawak’s flora and fauna through conservation and rehabilitation.
  • Promote eco-tourism and public awareness of environmental sustainability.

Our Vision

To be a leader in conservation, ensuring a sustainable future for nature and future generations.

Semenggoh Wildlife Centre

In 1975, Semenggoh Wildlife Centre was officially established within the 653 hectares of Semenggoh Nature Reserve tropical rainforest to cater for orphaned and abandoned orangutans as well as other wildlife species that were confiscated or surrendered by the local community.

Orangutans were rehabilitated before being released into the wild. Now, the Centre is responsible for managing semi-wild orangutans that live and roam within the Nature Reserve.

Besides that, it also caters for research, environmental education, and ecotourism activities.
